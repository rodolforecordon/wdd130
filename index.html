Rio de Janeiro, Brazil / Porto, Portugal

Portugal and Brazil are like father and son. Their shared heritage is undeniable: they speak the same language, though with different accents, and Portuguese architecture is found everywhere in Brazil. This common bond extends to the people, who in both countries are hardworking and very devoted to their families. In a twist of familial irony, this also includes a shared struggle with excessive bureaucracy.

True to a father-son dynamic, their relationship is complex, marked by both love and occasional frustration. Yet, for all their shared history, their natural worlds stand in stark contrast, with vastly different fauna and flora. Despite this, I have never met anyone who would deny that both are absolutely beautiful countries.
